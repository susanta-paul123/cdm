Our Story

Traditional Blended with Innovation with unwavering commitment to quality set from 250 years ago

Established over 250 years ago, Carr & Day & Martin boasts an exceptional heritage in the equestrian world. Our journey began back in 1765, with the launch of our enduring Belvoir Tack Conditioner soap in 1800, a formulation that has stood the test of time. The pivotal moment came in 1820 when we were granted the esteemed Royal Warrant by King George, an honour that has been retained through the reigns of 8 monarchs in the United Kingdom.

Today, we remain committed to upholding our legacy of quality craftsmanship and innovation, known worldwide for our renowned horse care products. While retaining our time-honoured traditional formulations, we also introduced a range for the modern world, ensuring we cater to the evolving needs of equestrians

Our mission

remains unwavering—to provide the finest horse care products, backed by centuries of expertise and a deep-rooted passion for equine grooming and care.

Timeline

1765

Established and started manufacturing in the UK

It all started in 1765 when Mr Day, a Doncaster hairdresser, met an old soldier who gave Day a recipe for making boot blacking which he had developed to polish the boots of his superior officers. “Make it like this” said Martin the solider “and you’ll make a fortune!” Very quickly Mr Day realised how good the product was and together they opened a little shop at 97, High Holburn, London in 1770.

1800

Belvoir soap first produced and still made to the same formula today

Mr Day had a flair for publicity and splendid black horses were used to draw the Day & Martin carriages. Apart from the horses themselves, the firm had wonderful stables and equipment. An interest in equestrian products thus ensued and the first equestrian product was made – the Belvoir Bar Soap. It remains the first glycerine bar soap on the market to this day.

1820

First Royal Warrant granted by King George IV and retained today through the reigns of 8 monarchs

1923

Day & Martins sell the company to Carr & Son

By this time they had established world-wide overseas trade and the product range now also included floor polish, harness composition, metal polish and ‘shoe and leather dressings of every kind’.

Sub Brands Uphold Quality Heritage through Time

With a legacy rooted in excellence, Carr Day Martin’s sub-brands exemplify the brand’s commitment to quality and innovation through the test of time.

Many of the sub-brands carry forward a heritage & legacy of exceptional quality for over 2 centuries. Belvoir Soap was first produced in 1800 and is still made with the same formulation today. We are proud of traditional brands such as Vanner & Prest Neatfoot, Breaknell Turner Soap, Cornuscrecine produced to the same high standards set back in 1765 that are favoured by generations of equestrian families as a stable in stables from the beginning.

Balancing Tradition with Innovative Modern Products

At Carr Day Martin, we hold steadfast to our commitment to traditional products while embracing the pride that comes with our newer innovations.

We take equal pride in offerings like our Gallop Shampoos, formulated to provide superior cleansing and coat care, and our Tack Mitt, a revolutionary bio-degradable tack cleaning mitt designed to efficiently clean and condition leather tack.
